Seller's Guide

How to prepare, price and close the sale of your property in Uganda — everything you need to know to get the best outcome.

Selling property in Uganda involves more than just finding a buyer. Getting the price right, having your documents in order, and understanding the transfer process all affect how quickly you sell — and how much you walk away with.

This guide walks you through every stage, so you can sell with confidence and without surprises.

Get your documents in order before listing. You'll need your original Certificate of Title, proof of ownership, any survey plans, and evidence of paid ground rent (for leasehold properties). Buyers and their lawyers will ask for these early — having them ready speeds everything up.

Get a market valuation before setting your asking price. Overpricing keeps buyers away; underpricing costs you money. A broker who knows your area can give you a realistic price range based on recent comparable sales in the neighbourhood.

Prepare the property for viewings. First impressions drive offers. Ensure the compound is clean and accessible, any outstanding repairs are addressed, and the interior is presented as well as possible. Properties with professional photographs consistently attract more serious inquiries.

List your property with a registered broker who will market it across the right channels — both online and through their network — and qualify buyers before bringing them to you. Your broker handles viewings, manages inquiries and keeps you updated without disrupting your daily routine.

Negotiate through your broker and agree on a price, payment terms and a realistic closing timeline. Once an offer is accepted, both parties should sign a Sale Agreement drafted or reviewed by a lawyer — never proceed on a handshake alone.

Work with a lawyer throughout the transfer process. The buyer's lawyer will conduct due diligence on the title. Your lawyer ensures the Sale Agreement protects your interests, handles the transfer of ownership documents at the Ministry of Lands, and confirms payment is received before the title is released.

Understand your tax obligations. Capital Gains Tax may apply depending on the nature of the property and how it was acquired. Speak to a tax advisor or the Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) before completing the sale so you know exactly what you'll owe.

Once the transfer is complete and you've received full payment, hand over all keys, access cards and any relevant documentation about the property — utility accounts, maintenance contracts, service provider contacts. This closes the transaction cleanly and professionally.
