Tenant's Guide

A simple guide to all the important information you need to know when renting in Kampala — from setting a budget to signing your tenancy agreement.

Renting a property in Kampala doesn't have to be stressful. Whether you're looking for a short-term furnished apartment or a long-term family home, knowing what to expect at each stage helps you find the right place at the right price — and protects you once you're in.

Set a realistic budget by considering all the features and amenities you need. The advertised rental price typically covers the base rent only and excludes additional costs such as: security deposit (usually 1–2 months' rent), agency fees, utilities connection deposits, and moving costs.

Work with a registered real estate broker who understands what you need and can find the right property within your budget. A good broker saves you time and helps you avoid listings that don't match what was advertised.

Attend viewings and ask questions. Go to every property you're seriously considering and ask about: the condition of plumbing and electrics, what's included in the rent (water, security, garbage), who handles maintenance, and how flexible the landlord is on terms.

Make an offer through your broker and review the tenancy agreement carefully before signing. Any additional terms or requirements should be added to the agreement in writing — a verbal promise from a landlord is not enforceable once you've signed.

Understand what your tenancy agreement covers — specifically the rent amount and payment schedule, notice period for either party, who is responsible for repairs and maintenance, rules on subletting or early termination, and what happens to your security deposit at the end.

Document the property's condition at move-in. Take dated photographs of every room, including any existing damage, and share them with your landlord before or on your move-in date. This protects your security deposit when you eventually move out.

Pay rent as agreed and keep records. Always pay through a documented channel (mobile money, bank transfer, or receive a signed receipt for cash payments). Never pay rent without documentation, regardless of how long you've known the landlord.

When you move out, give proper written notice as specified in your agreement, ensure the property is returned in the same condition it was rented to you (fair wear and tear excepted), and request your security deposit in writing. Any deductions must be itemized and justified by the landlord.
